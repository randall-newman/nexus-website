### Culture before tooling

AI-ready teams share habits: experimentation, documentation, and clear ownership of outcomes—not just access to new software.

- Encourage small pilots with measurable success criteria.
- Celebrate learning from failed experiments, not just wins.
- Pair technical and non-technical stakeholders on automation projects.

### Enablement that sticks

Training works best when tied to real workflows teams already run every week.

- Run hands-on workshops using live operational examples.
- Create internal playbooks for common automation patterns.
- Assign automation champions in each department.

### Conclusion

An AI-ready culture turns automation from a side project into a durable operating advantage.
